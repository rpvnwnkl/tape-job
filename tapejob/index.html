Busy Work

Let's face it: this is a working man's world.

The reality is that day after day, you show up, do your work, and go home. You push your pencil around, answering someone else's questions. You peck at your keyboard, balancing someone else's budget sheet. You're in here but the World's outside, buying and selling, dancing the dance of consumerism: puppets on the stage of capitalism.

And because your hands are guided as much as theirs, your role as servant in this financial system hangs over you like a deep grey rain cloud waiting to release. You don't create ideas; you follow instructions, protocols, and directions, so that the world outside can buy this, and use that, and continue to consume those ideas you don't create. You're in the middle, and your hands are guided.

Is your mind?
